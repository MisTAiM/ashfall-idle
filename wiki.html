Ashfall Idle — Overview

Ashfall Idle is a dark fantasy idle RPG where you train 28+ skills, battle 59 monsters across 12 combat areas, conquer dungeons, craft hundreds of items, manage a farm, pickpocket NPCs, and raise combat pets that fight alongside you. The world of Ashfall is covered in a grey volcanic fallout — a dark, gritty setting where survival means constant growth.

Getting Started

  1. Begin with Woodcutting, Mining, or Fishing to gather raw materials.
  2. Use Smithing to forge your first weapons and armor from ore. Use Cooking to prepare food for healing in combat.
  3. Head to Combat, equip your gear from the Bank, and fight Chickens or Goblins first.
  4. Use drops and XP to level combat skills. Upgrade weapons as you progress.
  5. Unlock Farming early — plants grow passively while you do other activities.
  6. Complete Quests for large XP rewards and faction reputation.
  7. Collect Combat Pets from monster drops — they fight alongside you.

Fishing

Catch raw fish for Cooking. Different fishing zones unlock at different levels, each offering different fish species. Higher-tier fish require level-appropriate rods.

Foraging

Gather wild herbs used in Alchemy (potions) and some cooking recipes. Higher-level herbs give more XP and are rarer.

Artisan Skills

Smithing

Smelt ores into bars at a furnace, then smith bars into weapons and armor. Each bar tier unlocks a new set of equipment. Artisan skill — requires input materials. Uses a progressive recipe unlock system.

Cooking

Cook raw fish and meat into food for combat healing. Higher Cooking level reduces burn rate. Some cooked foods restore more HP. Burnt food has no healing value.

Crafting

Create jewelry (rings, amulets), leather armor, and other items from gems, leather, and bars. Jewelry can be enchanted via Enchanting for combat bonuses.

Alchemy

Brew potions from herbs and ingredients. Potions provide temporary skill boosts, healing, or combat advantages. Some potions require herblore secondaries (e.g. fish oil, ashes).

Enchanting

Add magical properties to jewelry and weapons using runes and enchant dust. Enchanted items have stat bonuses beyond the base item. Requires runes appropriate to the enchantment tier.

Fletching

Craft arrows, bows, and bolts from logs, feathers, and metal bars. Ranged ammunition is consumed in combat. Higher-level bows deal more damage and have higher requirements.

Combat System

Ashfall uses a tick-based combat system. Both the player and the monster attack on independent timers. Combat ends when either party reaches 0 HP.

Damage Formula

How Damage is Calculated

maxRoll = weapon.maxHit + floor(strengthBonus / 5)

damage = random(0, maxRoll)

evasionChance = monster.evasion[style] / (monster.evasion[style] + 100)

Prayer bonuses and equipment bonuses are applied multiplicatively. Critical hits deal 1.5× damage (rare chance). Special attacks override the normal formula.

Special Attacks

Melee weapons can have special attacks costing Spec Energy. Energy regenerates at 10% per player attack (default). Equip special weapons and click the spec bar to trigger. Spec energy persists between fights.

Auto-Eat

When enabled (default ON), the game automatically eats your best food when your HP drops below 40%. Food is stored in the Food Bag (4 slots, accessible from Combat page). Add food by clicking food items in the Bank.

Combat Pets

Combat pets are companions that fight alongside you. Each pet has a combat ability that triggers every N player attacks, plus a passive bonus that is always active while the pet is equipped. Only one pet can be equipped at a time.

How Pet Combat Works

After every player attack, the game checks if your pet's ability counter has reached its trigger threshold. When it does, the pet's ability fires automatically — dealing damage, healing you, applying a status effect, or debuffing the monster.

The combat arena shows a small flash when your pet acts. The pet strip above the prayer bar shows your active pet and how often it acts.

Disease

At 50% growth, each plot rolls for disease. Base chance is 7-10% depending on plot type. Compost reduces this. A diseased plot turns dead and must be cleared with a Spade before replanting.

Weeds

Weeds randomly appear on growing plots. They slow growth. Use a Rake to remove them — this also speeds up remaining growth by 15%.

Yield Formula

qty = round(baseYield × compostMult × levelMult) + ultraExtra

Where levelMult = 1 + farmLevel/200 (up to +49.5% at 99). Seed return chance = 35% + compostTier×5%.

Thieving

Thieving lets you pickpocket NPCs and steal from market stalls for gold, XP, and rare items. It is dangerous — getting caught deals HP damage, and repeatedly failing angers your target until they fight back.

Core Mechanics

Stun System

Each pickpocket attempt has a stun chance (5%–50% depending on target). Getting caught:

  • Pauses your action for stunTime seconds
  • Deals HP damage: random(1, stunDmgBase) + target.level/10
  • Increases the target's Anger by 10–35% depending on target type

Anger System

Anger builds on each stun and decays slowly with successful pickpockets. High anger increases fight chance.

fightChance = (0.02 + targetLevel/90 × 0.08) + anger × 0.40

Example: A level 75 Knight at 50% anger has: (0.02 + 0.067) + 0.5×0.40 = ~29% fight chance per stun.

When a fight triggers, the target becomes a combat monster. Your HP is synced from thieving to combat — so if you were on low HP while thieving, you fight on low HP.

⚠ Death penalty: If your thieving HP reaches 0, you are knocked unconscious and lose 50% of your gold. HP restores to full after.

HP & Food

Thieving uses your combat HP pool (based on Hitpoints level). Your thieving HP bar is shown at the top of the Thieving page. Auto-eat triggers at 40% HP and uses food from your Food Bag. You can also manually eat by clicking food items in the Thieving status bar.

Mastery Bonus

Each mastery level in a specific pickpocket target reduces their effective stun chance by 0.3%. Mastery level 99 = -29.7% stun chance reduction, effectively halving the stun rate on most targets.

Fight Cave

The Fight Cave is a solo endgame challenge — 62 waves of increasingly dangerous monsters culminating in TzTok-Jad. Jad hits 97 per attack. Watch his animation to use the correct protection prayer. A Cape of Fire is the reward for first completion.

Key Formulas

XP to Level

XP_for_level_L = floor(sum(i=1 to L-1, floor(i + 300 × 2^(i/7))) / 4)

Level 99 = 13,034,431 XP.

Combat Level

CL = floor(0.25 × (defence + hitpoints + floor(prayer/2)) + 0.325 × max(attack+strength, ranged×2/3, magic×2/3))

Thieving Fight Chance

fightChance = min(0.75, (0.02 + targetLevel/90×0.08) + anger×0.40)

Farming Yield

qty = round(baseYield × (1+compostBonus) × (1+farmingLevel/200)) + ultraExtra

Pet Drop Rate

Pet drops are rolled after every kill/action. Rate varies by pet (listed in pet table). Mastery in the source skill does not affect drop rate — only volume of actions increases expected drops.

Prayer Drain

Prayer points drain after each player attack. Total drain = sum of all active prayers' pointCost values. At drain rate D, you last floor(prayerPoints / D) attacks on your prayer points pool.
