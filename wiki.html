Ashfall Idle Overview

Ashfall Idle is a dark fantasy idle RPG where you train 18 skills, fight over 15 unique monsters across 8 combat areas, conquer 7 dungeons, craft hundreds of items, and build your character from a humble adventurer into a legend. The game features full offline progression, a mastery system, farming, thieving, and deep interconnected skill chains.

Quick Start Guide

1. Start with Woodcutting, Mining, or Fishing to gather raw materials.

2. Use Smithing to forge weapons and armor. Use Cooking to prepare food for combat healing.

3. Equip your gear from the Bank, then head to Combat to fight monsters.

4. Defeat monsters to earn XP, gold, and rare loot. Progress to harder areas and dungeons.

5. Use Farming alongside other activities for passive resource generation.

6. Train all skills and complete achievements to master the world of Ashfall.

Game Mechanics

Mastery System

Every action in a non-combat skill has an individual mastery level (max 99). As mastery increases:

• Action time decreases by 0.5% per mastery level

• Cooking burn chance decreases by 0.5% per mastery level

• Thieving stun chance decreases by 0.3% per mastery level

Mastery XP is earned at 50% of the base action XP rate.

Combat System

Three combat styles: Melee (Attack + Strength), Ranged, and Magic. Your weapon determines your style.

Accuracy: Based on your offensive level and equipment bonus vs the monster's evasion.

Damage: Random between 1 and your max hit when an attack lands.

Food: Equip food from your bank. Auto-Eat triggers at 40% HP. Maximum 28 food stacked.

XP Distribution: Melee splits 40/40/20 (Atk/Str/Def). Ranged/Magic gives 80/20 (main/Def). All combat gives 33% to Hitpoints.

Offline Progression

When you close the game, your active skill continues training for up to 24 hours. Upon returning, all XP and items are calculated and awarded. Combat does not run offline. Farming timers run in real time.

Mining Gem Drops

While mining, there is a chance to find gems. The chance increases with higher-tier rocks (1% for Copper/Tin up to 5% for Obsidian). Gem rarity: Topaz (40%) > Sapphire (25%) > Ruby (15%) > Emerald (10%) > Diamond (8%) > Onyx (2%).

Equipment Slots

11 equipment slots: Weapon, Shield, Head, Body, Legs, Boots, Gloves, Ring, Amulet, Cape, Ammo. Each piece contributes stats that stack additively. Damage Reduction is a flat percentage applied after the hit calculation.

Formulas & Math

XP Table

Level progression follows the formula: XP[n] = XP[n-1] + floor((n + 300 * 2^(n/7)) / 4)

Level 10 = ~1,154 XP | Level 25 = ~8,740 XP | Level 50 = ~101,333 XP | Level 75 = ~1,210,421 XP | Level 99 = ~13,034,431 XP

Combat Formulas

Melee Accuracy: (Attack Level + 8) * (Attack Bonus + 64)

Melee Max Hit: floor(0.5 + Strength Level * (Strength Bonus + 64) / 640)

Evasion: (Defence Level + 8) * (Defence Bonus + 64)

Hit Chance: clamp(Accuracy / (Accuracy + Evasion), 0.05, 0.95)

Damage Reduction: Damage = max(1, floor(RawDamage * (100 - DR%) / 100))

Max HP: Hitpoints Level * 10 + 10

Combat Level: floor(0.25 * (Def + HP)) + max(0.325 * (Atk + Str), 0.325 * Rng * 1.5, 0.325 * Mag * 1.5)

Smithing Recipe Chain

Bronze Bar = 1 Copper + 1 Tin | Iron Bar = 1 Iron Ore | Steel Bar = 1 Iron + 2 Coal

Mithril Bar = 1 Mithril Ore + 4 Coal | Adamant Bar = 1 Adamant Ore + 6 Coal | Obsidian Bar = 1 Obsidian Ore + 8 Coal

Weapons require 2-4 bars depending on tier. Platebodies require 3-5 bars. Shields require 2-3 bars.
